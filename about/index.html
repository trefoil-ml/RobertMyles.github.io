About

My name is Robert McDonnell and I live in Brazil, in the hustle and bustle of São Paulo. I have studied Fine Art (in NCAD in Dublin, Ireland), International Relations & Political Science (in DCU), and I have just finished my PhD in International Relations in the University of São Paulo. My academic work has so far focused on Bayesian item-response models of legislative voting. Currently, I produce some research for the Getúlio Vargas Foundation, I’m (trying to) develop some R packages, and I give classes and courses on R and data analysis for IBPAD.

You can find my work on Github, or you can see my LinkedIn.
